In the ancient times, particularly in Greek and Roman civilizations, art was not displayed in dedicated galleries as we know them today. Instead, it was integrated into religious life. Primary displays of sculpture and painting were found in temples, and government buildings. This art served functional purposes to honor the gods, commemorate military victories, celebrate leaders, and decorate the spaces of the wealthy. Today the large number of ancient art can be seen in the original site of the archeological site across Italy and Rome. However, many of the pieces of the art is removed indoors for preservation.

Later in the middle age art was mainly displayed in the churches and many art gallery opened like (The Uffizi Gallery in Florence and The Louvre Museum in Paris) these gallery made art gallery accessible to all. After that many galleries and museums opened across the world to reflect their growing culture of art in their country. While art in the ancient world served as public entertainment in temples and other monuments, today it is displayed in dedicated art galleries and museums to reflect cultural and education. Art was shifted from religious devotion to culture prestige.
